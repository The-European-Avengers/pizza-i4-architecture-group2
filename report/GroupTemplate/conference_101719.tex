% Abstract
\begin{abstract}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%% Max 970 signs without space %%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
% Brief summary of the project, objectives, and key findings
This paper presents the design, formal verification, and experimental validation of a flexible, distributed architecture for Industry 4.0 manufacturing, demonstrated through a containerized pizza production testbed. The proposed system combines Event-Driven Architecture and Microservice-Oriented Architecture to achieve modularity, scalability, and real-time data processing. Formal verification using UPPAAL ensures deadlock freedom and compliance with timing constraints, while empirical experiments validate performance requirements under varying load conditions. The results confirm that the architecture meets key quality attributes, performance, availability, and integrability, while highlighting bottlenecks and proposing horizontal scaling strategies for future improvements.

\end{abstract}
